Bio

I am currently an optimization researcher in Electronics Research Department at Toyota Research Institute of North America (TRINA). My research focuses on exploring innovative geometries through simulation-based structural optimization to achieve high-performance, manufacturable engineering designs.

Previously, I spent one year in Japan working on photonic chip design with Toyota Central R&D Labs and Toyota Frontier Research Center. I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from University of Michigan. Before that, I completed my B.S. at Northeastern University (Shenyang, China).

Research

My works are at the intersection of design optimization, multiphysics simulation, and advanced fabrication. Recent example topics include:

  • Quantum photonic integrated circuits (NEW)
  • Lightweight composite structures
  • Flow field plates for hydrogen fuel cells
